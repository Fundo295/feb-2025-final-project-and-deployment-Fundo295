The World's Forests

Forests cover about 31% of the world's land area and contain most of Earth's terrestrial biodiversity.

Types of Forests

Forests can be classified into three main types: tropical, temperate, and boreal. Each has unique characteristics and supports different ecosystems.

Importance of Forests

  • Produce oxygen and absorb carbon dioxide
  • Regulate climate and water cycles
  • Provide habitat for countless species
  • Source of food, medicine, and materials

Conservation Efforts

Deforestation continues to threaten forests worldwide. Conservation programs focus on sustainable management, reforestation, and protection of endangered species.
